Bio

  • Nuria Ribas Costa is a Spanish Journalist and Jurist based between Rotterdam and Ibiza, her homeland. Formerly a member of the Public Relations team of OMA, she currently works as an independent researcher and policy analyst in the fields of urbanism and culture, with projects ranging from music resilience policy-making to collective governance of the city. Nuria leads the communications and citizen engagement research at Humankind, a Dutch agency for urban change, and collaborates closely with applied research team Vigla and the Center for Music Ecosystems. She is also an editor and moderator for De Dépendance, a co-founder of Revista 078 magazine and writes for independent media. Her work has been published in the European Review of Books, DAMN, FRAME, Failed Architecture, MONU and Are We Europe, among others. Nuria is interested in the relationships rural-urban, the politics of space and land and intersectionality in urbanism.
